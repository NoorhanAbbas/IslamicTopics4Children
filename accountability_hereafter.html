üìÖ Timeline: From Death to Eternity üìÖ

  • Your soul is extracted from your body
  • You see angels (beautiful for believers, terrifying for disbelievers)
  • You're shown your place in Paradise or Hell
  • The soul of a believer is taken up through the heavens
  • The soul of a disbeliever is rejected and cast down

  • Two angels come and ask three questions:
  • "Who is your Lord?" (Man rabbuka?)
  • "What is your religion?" (Ma deenuka?)
  • "Who is this man (the Prophet)?" (Man nabiyyuka?)

For Believers:

  • They answer correctly with firm conviction
  • Their grave becomes a garden from Paradise
  • They see their place in Paradise
  • They sleep peacefully and are visited by their good deeds

For Disbelievers:

  • They cannot answer or say "I don't know"
  • Their grave becomes a pit from Hell
  • They see their place in Hell
  • They are punished until the Day of Resurrection
3

The trumpet is blown, everyone who ever lived is resurrected, and all gather for the Day of Judgment.

  • Souls are reunited with bodies
  • People rise from their graves in the state they died
  • All creatures‚Äîhumans, jinn, animals‚Äîare gathered
  • The sun is brought extremely close causing intense heat
  • People sweat according to their sins (some up to ankles, some drowning in it)
4

What Happens During the Waiting:

  • People will be barefoot, naked, and uncircumcised (but too terrified to notice)
  • Some will have shade under Allah's Throne (the 7 categories)
  • People will desperately seek intercession
  • Prophet Muhammad Ô∑∫ will be granted permission to intercede
  • Records of deeds are distributed (right hand or left hand)
5

  • Your life‚Äîhow you spent it
  • Your youth‚Äîhow you used it
  • Your wealth‚Äîhow you earned and spent it
  • Your knowledge‚Äîwhat you did with it
  • Your body‚Äîhow you utilized it

Witnesses Against You:

  • Your own limbs will testify against you
  • The earth will testify about what you did on it
  • Angels who recorded every deed
  • The Prophets and righteous will testify
6

‚öñÔ∏è The Scales (Al-Mizan)

Your deeds are weighed on the scales. Every atom of good and bad is measured.

How It Works:

  • Good deeds are placed on one side, bad deeds on the other
  • Even the smallest deeds are weighed
  • Some deeds are incredibly heavy (like saying "SubhanAllah wa bihamdihi")
  • Some sins are incredibly heavy (like shirk, oppression)
  • Those whose good deeds outweigh will succeed
  • Those whose bad deeds outweigh will face punishment
7

  • There are hooks along the bridge that grab people based on their sins
  • Prophet Muhammad Ô∑∫ and other Prophets will be there making du'a
  • The first to cross will be Prophet Muhammad Ô∑∫ and his followers
8

üè∞ Final Destination: Jannah or Jahannam

After crossing the Sirat, believers enter Paradise. Those who fall enter Hell. Both are eternal.

‚ö†Ô∏è Major and Minor Sins ‚ö†Ô∏è

  • Disobeying parents

  • Gossiping without serious harm
  • Wasting time excessively
  • Looking at what's forbidden (glances)
  • Small acts of dishonesty
  • Breaking promises
  • Being impatient or angry (without harm)
  • Laziness in worship

‚ö†Ô∏è Warning: Minor sins done repeatedly can become major! And major sins done publicly harm the whole community.

‚úÖ Conditions for Valid Repentance

Before death or before the sun rises from the west (a major sign of the Day of Judgment).

üíÄ Living with Consciousness of Death üíÄ

  • Motivates you to repent quickly
  • Makes you kinder and more forgiving
  • Helps you avoid sins (ask yourself: What if I die doing this?)
  • Makes you appreciate your blessings
  • Encourages you to prepare for the afterlife
  • Softens your heart

üìø Practical Ways to Remember Death

  • Make du'a for a good death and ending

  • Imagine yourself being questioned in the grave

üí° The Wise Balance üí°

Remember death enough to motivate you to do good and avoid evil, but not so much that you become paralyzed with fear or neglect your worldly duties. The Prophet Ô∑∫ said: "Work for this world as if you'll live forever, and work for the Hereafter as if you'll die tomorrow."
