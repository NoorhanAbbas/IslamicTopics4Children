üì¢ Enjoining Good and Forbidding Evil üì¢

üéØ A Core Responsibility

One of the defining characteristics of the Muslim Ummah is that we encourage each other toward good and discourage evil. This is not optional‚Äîit's an obligation upon every Muslim according to their ability.

"You are the best nation produced for mankind. You enjoin what is right and forbid what is wrong and believe in Allah." - Quran 3:110

‚öñÔ∏è The Three Levels of Action

The Prophet Ô∑∫ taught us three levels of responding to evil, in order of priority:

  • 1. With Your Hand (Physical Action): For those in positions of authority‚Äîparents, leaders, officials. Stop the evil directly if you have the power and authority to do so.
  • 2. With Your Tongue (Speech): Speak up against the evil. Advise, counsel, remind. This is what most people should do.
  • 3. With Your Heart (Internal Rejection): At minimum, hate the evil in your heart and don't participate. This is the weakest level of faith.

üí° How to Enjoin Good and Forbid Evil WITH WISDOM

Essential Guidelines:

  • Private First: Advise people privately before publicly. Preserve their dignity.
  • Be Gentle and Kind: Use soft words, show compassion. Harshness pushes people away.
  • Start with Yourself: Practice what you preach. Don't be a hypocrite.
  • Have Knowledge: Make sure what you're forbidding is actually wrong and what you're commanding is actually obligatory.
  • Consider Consequences: Don't cause greater harm while trying to fix lesser harm.
  • Be Patient: Change takes time. Don't give up if people don't listen immediately.
  • Right Intention: Do it for Allah's sake, not to show off or feel superior.
  • Wisdom and Timing: Choose the right time and place. Don't embarrass people publicly.

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå Being Harsh and Judgmental: Turning people away from Islam with harshness
‚ùå Public Shaming: Exposing people's sins publicly
‚ùå Focusing on Minor Issues: Making a big deal about small matters while ignoring major sins
‚ùå Not Practicing It Yourself: Commanding others while you don't do it
‚ùå Lacking Knowledge: Forbidding what's permissible or allowing what's forbidden
‚ùå Being Selective: Only correcting people you don't like while ignoring your friends' mistakes

üí¨ Giving Sincere Advice (Nasiha) üí¨

The Prophet Ô∑∫ said: "Religion is sincerity (nasiha)." When asked "To whom?", he replied: "To Allah, His Book, His Messenger, the leaders of the Muslims, and the common people."

1

Sincere Intention

Give advice purely for the benefit of the person and to please Allah, not to show off your knowledge or feel superior.

2

Private, Not Public

Advise people privately to protect their dignity. Public advice often leads to defensiveness and embarrassment. "Advise privately, praise publicly."

3

Gentle Approach

Use kind words, soft tone, and show genuine care. The Prophet Ô∑∫ said: "Whoever is deprived of gentleness is deprived of all good."

4

Right Time and Place

Choose when the person is receptive. Not when they're angry, stressed, or in front of others. Timing matters!

5

Focus on the Issue, Not the Person

Address the behavior, not attack their character. "This action is problematic" not "You're a bad person."

6

Listen to Their Side

After giving advice, listen to their perspective. They might have reasons or context you're not aware of. Be willing to admit if you were wrong.

7

Don't Force Acceptance

Your job is to advise sincerely, not to force them to change. After you've given good advice, leave the matter. Guidance is from Allah.

8

Make Du'a for Them

After advising, make sincere du'a for the person that Allah guides them and makes it easy for them to change.

üö´ Forbidden Speech That Destroys Community üö´

1Ô∏è‚É£ Backbiting (Gheebah)

"Mentioning your brother in a way he would dislike (behind his back)."

The Prophet Ô∑∫ defined backbiting clearly: even if what you say is TRUE, if you say it behind someone's back and they wouldn't like it, it's backbiting.

Examples:

  • "Did you see how she dresses? Not very modest..."
  • "He's so lazy, he never gets anything done on time"
  • "She's gained so much weight lately"
  • "His recitation is terrible, he makes so many mistakes"

‚ö†Ô∏è The Quran compares backbiting to eating your dead brother's flesh! (49:12) This shows how disgusting it is to Allah.

2Ô∏è‚É£ Slander (Buht an)

"Saying something false about someone that they would dislike."

This is even worse than backbiting‚Äîyou're lying AND harming their reputation. This is a major sin.

Examples:

  • Spreading false rumors about someone
  • Accusing someone of something they didn't do
  • Exaggerating their faults or adding things that aren't true

3Ô∏è‚É£ Tale-Bearing (Nameemah)

"Conveying speech from one person to another to cause harm between them."

"Did you know what he said about you? He said..." This creates enmity, breaks relationships, and destroys community trust.

‚ö†Ô∏è The Prophet Ô∑∫ said: "The tale-bearer will not enter Paradise." This shows its severity!

‚úÖ Exceptions: When Can You Mention Someone's Faults?

In specific situations, mentioning someone's faults is permissible:

  • Seeking help to stop oppression: "This person is oppressing me, I need help"
  • Asking for Islamic ruling: "My husband does X, is this allowed?"
  • Warning others from harm: "Don't do business with him, he cheated me"
  • Public sinner: Someone openly sinning with no shame
  • Identifying someone: "The tall brother" or "the one who limps" (if no other way)

üõ°Ô∏è Covering Faults vs. Addressing Harm üõ°Ô∏è

‚úÖ Cover Others' Faults

The general principle is to cover the faults of Muslims and not expose their sins.

  • "Whoever covers a Muslim, Allah will cover him in this world and the Hereafter" - Prophet Ô∑∫
  • Don't spread people's mistakes and sins
  • Give them the benefit of the doubt
  • If you see a private sin, keep it private
  • Advise them privately, don't expose them
  • Make du'a for them, don't gossip about them

‚ö†Ô∏è Address Harmful Behavior

However, if someone's behavior is harming others or the community, it must be addressed appropriately.

  • If someone is abusing others, report to authorities
  • If spreading wrong teachings, scholars must warn
  • If someone is a danger, warn those at risk
  • Public sins harming the community need addressing
  • Don't cover for oppressors or criminals

üí° The Balance üí°

Cover people's private sins and mistakes. But don't cover for oppression, abuse, or public harm. There's a difference between protecting someone's dignity and enabling harm to others. Use wisdom!

  • If you witness injustice, speak up‚Äîeven if the oppressor is your friend

‚ö†Ô∏è False Testimony is a Major Sin
