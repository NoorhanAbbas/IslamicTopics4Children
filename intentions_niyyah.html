üíé Sincerity (Ikhlas) vs. Showing Off (Riya') üíé

‚úÖ Ikhlas (Sincerity)

  • Doing things solely for Allah's pleasure
  • Not caring if people see or praise you
  • Happy to do good deeds in private
  • Same effort in worship whether alone or with people
  • Don't mention your good deeds constantly
  • Feel uncomfortable when praised
  • Your motivation is Allah's reward, not people's approval
‚ú® Result: Actions accepted, huge rewards, peace of heart

‚ùå Riya' (Showing Off)

  • Doing good deeds to be seen and praised
  • Praying better when people are watching
  • Giving charity publicly to gain reputation
  • Mentioning your good deeds to others
  • Feeling proud when people praise you
  • Less motivated when alone
  • Your goal is people's admiration, not Allah's pleasure
‚ö†Ô∏è Result: Actions rejected, no reward, possible punishment!

‚ö†Ô∏è The Danger of Showing Off (Riya') ‚ö†Ô∏è

üî• Riya' is Hidden Shirk

The Prophet Ô∑∫ said: "What I fear most for you is minor shirk (associating partners with Allah)." When asked what it was, he said: "Riya' (showing off)."

Why? Because you're doing the action partly for Allah and partly for people's approval‚Äîyou've made people's praise a partner alongside Allah's pleasure!

üíî Your Good Deeds Can Be Thrown in Your Face

The Prophet Ô∑∫ said the first three people judged on the Day of Judgment will include:

  • A scholar who learned to be called a scholar‚Äîthrown into the Fire
  • A warrior who fought to be called brave‚Äîthrown into the Fire
  • A wealthy person who gave charity to be praised‚Äîthrown into the Fire

They did good deeds but for the wrong reason. Allah will tell them: "You got your reward already (people's praise in the world)."

üõ°Ô∏è How to Protect Yourself from Riya'

  • Keep many good deeds secret‚Äîknown only to you and Allah
  • Check your intention before, during, and after actions
  • Don't tell people about your good deeds unless necessary
  • Feel uncomfortable with praise, redirect it to Allah
  • When people praise you, say "Alhamdulillah" and move on
  • Ask yourself: Would I still do this if no one would ever know?

üïå Intention in Worship vs. Worldly Matters üåç

üïå Acts of Worship

  • Must be for Allah alone: Prayer, fasting, hajj, charity‚Äîthese require pure intention for Allah
  • Any show-off invalidates it: Even a trace of riya' in worship is dangerous
  • Specific intention needed: You must intend to pray Dhuhr (not just pray), fast Ramadan (not just fast)
  • Can't have mixed intentions: Can't pray to show off AND for Allah
  • Examples: Salah, sawm, zakah, hajj, dhikr, reciting Quran

üåç Worldly Matters

  • Can have mixed intentions: Work for Allah's pleasure AND to support family‚Äîboth are good!
  • Worldly benefit is okay: Studying for knowledge AND good career‚Äîboth fine
  • Primary intention matters: As long as pleasing Allah is your main goal, worldly benefits are okay
  • Transform to worship with intention: Normal activities become worship when done with right intention
  • Examples: Working, studying, marriage, eating, sleeping, exercising

‚öñÔ∏è The Key Principle ‚öñÔ∏è

For pure acts of worship, your intention must be exclusively for Allah. For worldly matters, you can intend both pleasing Allah and gaining worldly benefit, as long as pleasing Allah is your primary intention and the worldly pursuit is halal.

üí° Practical Steps to Improve Your Intentions üí°

1

Every morning when you wake up, say: "Today, I intend everything I do for Allah's sake." This frames your entire day as worship.

2

Before any significant action, pause for 2-3 seconds and clarify your intention silently in your heart.

3

Weekly or monthly, review your major activities and ask: "Why am I really doing this?" Be honest with yourself.

4

Regularly pray: "O Allah, make my intention pure for You alone. Protect me from riya' and grant me ikhlas."

5

Deliberately do some good deeds that NO ONE knows about except Allah. This trains your heart in sincerity.

6

When praised, immediately say "Alhamdulillah" and thank Allah in your heart. Don't dwell on people's words.

7

Study Lives of the Pious

Read about the Companions and righteous predecessors to see examples of pure intention and sincerity.

8

Don't Announce Everything

Not every good deed needs to be shared on social media or told to others. Some things are just between you and Allah.
