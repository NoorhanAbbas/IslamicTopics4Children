1. Lower the Gaze (Ghadd al-Basr)

Both men and women are commanded to lower their gaze when interacting with non-mahram. This doesn't mean never looking at someone's face when necessary, but avoiding lustful or prolonged staring.

  • What it means: Look briefly when needed for legitimate purposes (work, education, medical), then look away
  • Avoid: Staring, gazing at attractive features, or looking with desire
  • Prophet's advice Ô∑∫: "O Ali, do not follow a glance with another, for you are forgiven the first, but not the second"
üó£Ô∏è

2. Appropriate Speech

Communication should be clear, respectful, and purposeful without unnecessary softness, flirtation, or inappropriate topics.

  • Speak professionally: Direct, respectful, and to the point
  • Avoid softening the voice: The Quran warns against this specifically (33:32)
  • No flirtation: Avoid compliments about appearance, romantic topics, or suggestive language
  • Stick to necessary topics: Work, education, legitimate needs
ü§ù

3. Physical Contact (Khalwah Avoidance)

Avoid unnecessary physical contact and being alone together (khalwah) in private settings.

  • No handshaking: The Prophet Ô∑∫ never shook hands with non-mahram women
  • No khalwah: Don't be alone together in a secluded place. "Satan is the third" when non-mahram are alone together
  • Maintain distance: Keep appropriate physical space during interactions
  • Public settings: Interactions should be in presence of others or observable by others
üëó

4. Modest Dress (Hijab & Covering)

Both men and women must dress modestly when in the presence of non-mahram.

  • For women: Hijab covering hair, loose-fitting clothing covering body except face and hands
  • For men: Cover from navel to knee minimum, avoid tight or revealing clothing
  • Purpose: Prevent attraction and maintain dignity
  • Both genders: Avoid perfume/scent that may attract opposite gender
üíº

5. Professional Boundaries

Maintain professional relationships without crossing into personal friendship territory.

  • Keep it professional: Work/study collaborations are fine with proper conduct
  • Avoid unnecessary socializing: No casual hangouts, coffee dates, or personal time together
  • Group settings are safer: If socializing is necessary, do so in groups
  • No exclusive friendships: Close personal friendships with non-mahram should be avoided
