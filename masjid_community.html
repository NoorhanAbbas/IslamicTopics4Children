ğŸ¯ Etiquette of the Masjid

3. Use Perfume (Avoiding Strong Scents) Men should use pleasant but not overpowering fragrance. Avoid strong perfumes that might disturb others.
4. Avoid Eating Foods with Strong Odors Don't eat garlic, onions, or strong-smelling foods before going to the masjid. The Prophet ï·º specifically prohibited this to avoid disturbing others and the angels.

âš–ï¸ The Rights of the Masjid Upon You

Just as we have rights, the masjid has rights upon us as believers. Fulfilling these rights is part of honoring Allah's houses.

1. Attend Regular Prayers (Especially Fajr and Isha) The Prophet ï·º emphasized the importance of praying in congregation, particularly Fajr and Isha. These prayers in the masjid are signs of strong faith and earn multiplied rewards.
2. Attend Jumu'ah Prayer Friday prayer is obligatory for men. Missing it without valid excuse is a major sin. The Prophet ï·º warned: "Whoever misses three Jumu'ah prayers out of negligence, Allah will place a seal upon his heart."
3. Keep It Clean and Beautiful Cleanliness is part of faith. Help maintain the masjid's cleanliness - pick up trash, organize shoes, clean the wudu area, vacuum carpets. The Prophet ï·º said removing harm from the path is charity - how much more so in Allah's house!
4. Support It Financially The masjid needs funds for utilities, maintenance, programs, and staff. Regular financial contributions are essential. Remember: "Whoever builds a masjid for Allah, Allah builds for him a house in Paradise."
5. Volunteer Your Time and Skills Offer to teach classes, help with youth programs, assist with events, or use your professional skills for the masjid's benefit. Every hour volunteered is an investment in the Hereafter.
6. Defend Its Honor and Sanctity If you see anyone disrespecting the masjid or violating its sanctity, politely remind them (with wisdom). Don't allow Allah's house to be treated carelessly.
7. Make It a Place of Unity, Not Division Don't bring political disputes, ethnic tensions, or personal conflicts into the masjid. The masjid should unite hearts, not divide them.
8. Bring Your Family (Encourage Children and Spouses) Make the masjid a family affair. Bring children to familiarize them with the house of Allah, and support women's access to the masjid when they wish to attend.
Important Reflection: Ask yourself honestly - when was the last time you volunteered at your masjid? When did you last donate specifically for its needs? When did you last attend a program beyond just the five daily prayers? The masjid gives you so much - what are you giving back?

ğŸ“– Women in the Prophet's Time
